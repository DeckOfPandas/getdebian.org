What is Debian?


Debian is a stable & easy-to-use operating system for all of your laptop, desktop and server systems.

An operating system is the set of basic programs and utilities that make your computer run.

  • Free for any purpose

    Debian is free for anyone to use, modify, and distribute without charge.

  • Stable, secure & reliable

    Debian is trusted by millions of people across the globe, from solo backpackers to astronauts on the International Space Station, small companies to huge organisations.

  • Worldwide community

    Debian is built by an all-volunteer worldwide organisation and is available in over 70 languages,

History of Debian

The Debian Project began in 1993, when Ian Murdock issued an open invitation to software developers to contribute to a complete and coherent software distribution based on the relatively new Linux kernel.

That relatively small band of dedicated enthusiasts has grown over the years into a volunteer organization of well over 1,000 Debian Developers distributed across the world and collaborating over the internet.

Free software

But that's not all. Every part of Debian is free software. Whilst Debian is distributed without charge, "free software" is a matter of liberty, not price: think "free" as in "free speech".

This means you can…

  • Run any program as you want for any purpose.
  • Study how Debian works & change it so it does what you wish.
  • Redistribute copies of Debian..
  • Dstribute copies of your modified versions of Debian so others may benefit from changes.
